A little bit about me

My parents are from China but came to Canada for jobs/post-doctoral work where I was born, and although I think I still have a Canadian citizenship I moved either a day before or after I turned a year old. Needless to say I don't exactly consider myself Canadian and remember almost nothing from Canada. After that I spent 8 years in Oregon, until about a third of the way through 3rd grade. After that I've spent the rest of my life in California.

Most recently I went to Goleta for a year to serve as a full-time ministry intern for a church on the UCSB campus. The church was only in its second year so there was a lot of work to do to build it up (from mentoring to being an audio engineer to cooking), but it was a also a privilege to see a church growing in its younger years and to be a part of establishing the culture there and seeing the students, Christian and non-Christian alike, grow in their understanding and appreciation of Christianity. And now I'm looking forward to starting this bootcamp with you guys and hope to learn a lot in my time here!

What I'm up to now

I just moved back to Berkeley from Goleta two months ago, and now in my spare time I volunteer as a campus minister to graduate students and international students on the UC Berkeley campus, a little different from the undergraduates I was with at UCSB, trying to teach them about Christianity. In my work as a campus minister I've interacted with students from Peru, Nigeria, China, Japan, Korea, India, and several others. Personally I became Christian through a campus ministry group my freshmen year in college and it's changed my life in tremendous ways (positively) so being able to volunteer as a campus minister and share the same things I learned in college (outside of my classes) with students from all over the world (although most of them are from China) has been a tremendous privilege.

If you have any questions about Christianity or feel like talking about religion in general I'm totally down to dialogue with you. Exploring other people's beliefs and being able to share my own beliefs with them is one of my greatest joys in life and I think one of the best parts of being in an area as diverse as the Bay Area is the diversity of worldviews that's present here.
